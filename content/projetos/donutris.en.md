This may not seem like one of the most impressive models, but it has a pretty cool story behind it.

In 2022, Andrew Price, better known as [Blender Guru](https://www.youtube.com/@blenderguru), the biggest Blender tutorial channel on YouTube, created a collaborative campaign to raise money for the [Blender Foundation](https://www.blender.org/about/foundation/), and as I really admire the free software initiative, and owe a lot of what I know today to Andrew, I decided to contribute.

</br>
<iframe padding="100px" class="video" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/jv3GijkzIdk?si=D-Wo5tgie9usls8z" title="YouTube video player" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; clipboard-write; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture; web-share" referrerpolicy="strict-origin-when-cross-origin" allowfullscreen></iframe>

The campaign asked each artist who wanted to take part to make a 3D doughnut, inspired by Andrew's tutorials, which have become so popular that they are almost a trademark of those learning 3D modeling in Blender, to the point of being referenced in the wonderful film [Everything Everywhere All at Once](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/andrew-price-17678911_apparently-my-donut-tutorial-influenced-the-activity-7033524636797595648-pWB0/), winner of seven Oscars, including best picture.

![](https://staticg.sportskeeda.com/editor/2023/03/176e1-16793777025581-1920.jpg)

Then he gathered the work of all 17,731 contributing artists into a [single piece](https://andrewprice.art/17731-first-steps) to be auctioned off, and selected the 30 best donuts to be auctioned off individually, and I was [one of the chosen ones!](https://opensea.io/assets/ethereum/0x495f947276749ce646f68ac8c248420045cb7b5e/52497038234800833041189910550809680081297599854390804766694516516381234561025)
